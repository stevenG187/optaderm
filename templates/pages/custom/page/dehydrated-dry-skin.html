Dehydrated & Dry Skin

What is Dehydrated Skin?

Dehydrated skin is a condition where the skin lacks water. Unlike dry skin, which is characterized by a lack of oil, dehydrated skin is specifically short on moisture. This results in a dull appearance, fine lines, and often dark under-eye circles. Dehydrated skin can affect anyone, regardless of skin type, and it’s temporary and treatable.

What is Dry Skin?

Dry skin occurs when the skin lacks sufficient oils (lipids) to retain moisture. Unlike dehydrated skin, which lacks water, dry skin is characterized by a lack of natural oils that lock in hydration. As a result, the skin often appears dull, flaky, or rough in texture and may feel tight.

How to Scientifically Treat Dehydrated Skin?

Dehydrated skin requires both internal and external approaches:

  • Internal Hydration: Drink plenty of water to replenish moisture from within. Hydration starts from inside the body.
  • External Hydration: Use products that focus on water retention in the skin. Optaderm’s moisturizers, featuring Hyaluronic Acid, are key to instant hydration. Hyaluronic Acid holds water molecules in the skin, giving it a plump and hydrated look.
  • Proper Cleansing: A proper skincare routine starts with cleansing. Optaderm’s cleansers contain Cationic Hyaluronic Acid, which bonds to the skin’s surface for immediate hydration.
  • Toning: After cleansing, toners with Hyaluronic Acid can deliver additional moisture to the skin.
  • Moisturization: Sealing hydration with a suitable moisturizer is critical. Optaderm’s range includes lightweight gels for oily skin and richer creams for those who need extra moisture.

How to Scientifically Treat Dry Skin?

Treating dry skin involves restoring the skin’s oil balance and preventing further moisture loss:

  • Use Oil-Rich Cleansers: Start with gentle cleansers like Optaderm’s Cushion Emollient Cream Face Wash or Respect Hydrophilic Oil Facial Cleansing Oil. These products cleanse without stripping the skin’s natural oils.
  • Apply Toners: Hydrating toners with moisture-boosting ingredients are essential after cleansing to prepare the skin for further treatment.
  • Use Rich Serums and Creams: Optaderm’s Repair Lipid Ceramide Barrier Repair Serum and Peptide-rich moisturizers help restore the skin’s lipid barrier, locking in moisture and providing a smooth texture.
  • Lubrication and Oils: Incorporate oils, esters, and butters into your skincare routine to create a protective layer that locks in moisture.
  • Laser Therapy: Fractional 1540 and Q-Switch YAG PTP Fractional Laser therapies can be used to strengthen the skin’s dermal layer, promoting moisture retention and a smoother texture.
