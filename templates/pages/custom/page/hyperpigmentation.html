HYPERPIGMENTATION

What is Hyperpigmentation?

Hyperpigmentation is a condition where certain areas of the skin become darker than the surrounding skin due to an excess of melanin. Melanin is the pigment that gives skin its natural colour, and it resides in cells called melanocytes in the base layer of the epidermis. When melanocytes are damaged or become overactive, they produce excess melanin, resulting in dark patches. These areas of discoloration are commonly referred to as age spots, sunspots, or liver spots.

How to Scientifically Treat Hyperpigmentation?

Treating hyperpigmentation involves targeting the melanocyte activity to reduce excess melanin production. At Optaderm, we utilize scientifically proven ingredients such as:

  • Peptides (Palmitoyl Pentapeptide-4 and Tetrapeptide-30): These peptides help regulate melanin production and promote an even skin tone.
  • Tranexamic Acid: A powerful ingredient that reduces hyperpigmentation by inhibiting the overproduction of melanin.
  • Niacinamide: This form of Vitamin B3 helps brighten the skin and reduce the appearance of dark spots by suppressing the transfer of melanin to the surface of the skin.

Additionally, maintaining a good skincare routine and protecting your skin from UV exposure are crucial in managing hyperpigmentation. Sun protection is key because pigmentation spots can return with repeated sun exposure.
