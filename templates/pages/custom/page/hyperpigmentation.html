HYPERPIGMENTATION

Skin Concern

Skin gets its colour from a pigment called melanin, which resides in the base layer of the epidermis in cells called melanocytes. Over time, or when these melanocytes are damaged, permanent pigment patches may appear. These spots, often referred to as age spots, sunspots, or liver spots, can diminish the skinâ€™s natural reflective quality, giving it an aged appearance. Although these pigmented areas are not itchy or painful, they can cause individuals tofeel self-conscious.

Common causes of hyperpigmentation include aging (typically over 50), excessive sun exposure, and trauma, such as Post Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation (PIHP). Another form of pigmentation, more common in women, is melasma, which appears as brown to gray-brown patches on the face. Melasma is often triggered by pregnancy, contraceptives, or hormonal imbalances, which can damage melanocytes.
