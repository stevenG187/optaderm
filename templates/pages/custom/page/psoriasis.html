What is Psoriasis?

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that causes rapid skin cell turnover, leading to the development of reddish-pink raised skin lesions covered with silvery scales. These patches can appear anywhere on the body and are often itchy or painful. Psoriasis occurs when the immune system, particularly T-cells, mistakenly attacks healthy skin cells, accelerating their production and leading to an accumulation of new skin cells on the surface.

How to Scientifically Manage Psoriasis?

Psoriasis requires medical treatment, but certain skincare routines can help manage symptoms by improving the skin’s barrier function and reducing discomfort:

  • Barrier Repair: Optaderm products, such as Repair Lipid Ceramide Barrier Repair Serumm, help reinforce the skin’s barrier, which is critical in reducing irritation and supporting skin health.
  • Moisturizing: Consistent use of moisturizers like Comfort Facial Balm can help keep the skin hydrated, reduce flakiness, and alleviate itching.
  • Sun Protection: Protecting the skin from UV rays is essential, as sun exposure can worsen psoriasis symptoms. In some cases, your healthcare professional may recommend UVB light therapy, a controlled form of sun exposure.
  • Avoid Exfoliation: While exfoliation is often used for dry skin, it can cause discomfort and irritation for individuals with psoriasis, as the skin is already sensitive and inflamed.
