Skincare Through the Ages

Let’s talk about the term ‘anti-aging’ in skincare

The cosmetics industry has put themselves in the corner with the term "anti-aging".

The term was ‘dreamed up’ in the 1980s by advertising executives to sell products to the aging customer. If the customer could be convinced aging was a condition that needs to be battled, they would be scared and shamed into buying their products.

Some companies and publications started questioning this term around 2017, and have requested their advertisers stop altogether with the term. Growing older is inevitable.

My personal view was that the only honest use of the term anti-aging could mean; if you used certain products for 30 to 40 years; you would look better than if you did not. This can be a tall promise with a long time to see results. The best way to advise the customer is to educate them on what they are doing and why. This has been my mission since day one.

New active ingredients arrive daily, I look at many and use the science reviewed ingredients that help the skin with all aspects of aging, with its myriad of problems and conditions. Optaderm addresses your skin’s function – which leads to looking your best in your natural state. This conversation is NOT about cosmetic surgery, botox, fillers - but your actual skin function. If you want to pursue true medical enhancements and interventions to your appearance you still need to address your actual underlying skin fitness.

"Aging is a privilege" if you can appreciate what is happening to you as you age. Growing old gives you a new chance every day, to live a full life, something many don't get the opportunity to do. Hopefully, this entails skincare to keep you looking good at every stage of your life. Optaderm is here to address your skin’s function.

Aging skin is caused by 2 main factors

1

Intrinsic Aging

Natural Aging

Genetics play a key role in determining how efficiently your skin produces essential proteins like collagen and elastin, which are vital for maintaining firmness and elasticity. They also influence your skin’s ability to retain moisture and repair damage.

2

Extrinsic Aging

Environmental Factors

Sun exposure (the leading cause) which breaks down collagen.
Pollution, smoking, and poor diet which contribute to oxidative stress and skin damage.
Alcohol and stress can also lead to dehydration and inflammation.
Lack of sleep hinders skin repair, causing dullness and fine lines.

Together, these factors result in wrinkles, sagging, and other signs of aging.

As your skin matures, there are products with active ingredients, and various aesthetic services and laser therapies to specifically address the health of your skin through the aging processes. Support your skin’s natural aging process by stimulating its ability to produce and maintain collagen, elastin, and hyaluronic acid for a healthier, more resilient complexion.

Let's start with products with the right active ingredients

As collagen, hyaluronic acid and elastin production slows, start using peptides, ceramides, and saccharides to signal production of these building blocks in our skin.

In addition to a solid basic skin care routine, start using peptide treatment products such as Optaderm’s Profound, Revitalize, and Transform Peptide Facial Concentrates to address these conditions.

Retinoic Acid is also very helpful for helping the skin become smoother, diminishing wrinkles, and has certain pigment lightening features. The cosmetic industry generally uses Retinol, which is gentler than applying pure Retinoic Acid.
